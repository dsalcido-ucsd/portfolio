Proposition: The NYPD has a systemic misconduct problem that requires reform.

FOR the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

Transformations

  • De-duplicate to officer–complaint pairs; define “proven” as any CCRB-substantiated variant.
  • Rank officers by proven counts, then bin into 20 equal 5% groups (ventiles).
  • Compute each ventile’s % of all proven links and the lift vs 5% fair-share (top 5% = 13.6% ⇒ 2.7×).

Marks, Encodings & Visual Design

  • Use bar length (most accurate) with a 5% baseline for instant “fair-share” comparison.
  • Single-hue sequential ramp only above 5%; ≤5% bars stay neutral gray → outliers feel dominant without altering scales.
  • Extra headroom prevents label collisions; arrow + value highlight just the top bar.

Titles, Labels & Annotations

  • Action title frames the claim (Top 5%… 2.7× their fair share).
  • Subtitle states methods on the plot (rank, ventiles, “proven” definition).
  • Footnote clarifies unit (officer), de-duplication, and universe (officers ever appearing in CCRB records).

AGAINST the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

Transformations

  • Restrict to closed complaints (exclude open/unknown).
  • Recode outcomes to two groups: Not substantiated (Exonerated + Unsubstantiated) vs Substantiated (all variants).
  • Compute shares, ≈3.0× ratio, and “≈1 in 4 substantiated.”

Marks, Encodings & Visual Design

  • Minimalist single 100% bar emphasizes composition; internal split preserves detail (Exonerated vs Unsubstantiated).
  • Calm teal for Not-substantiated (favored) vs muted peach for Substantiated (downplayed) — persuasive but not garish.
  • 50% dashed line aids reading; badge calls out “≈1 in 4 substantiated.”

Titles, Labels & Annotations

  • Title delivers the stance (Not-substantiated outcomes are ≈3.0× as common…).
  • Subtitle states the exact recode on-plot; footnote reiterates mapping.
  • On-bar labels use contrast-safe strokes; micro-labels placed below to avoid crowding.
